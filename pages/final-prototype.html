Final Prototype

A quick description of the final prototype of our project, what we learned from the experience, and what we'd do differently moving forward.

Learn More
Early Concepts
Demo Video and Operation In the video demonstration, the roller mechanism operates through a high-torque stepper motor integrated with a drive screw. Upon reaching the end of the track, the carriage triggers a limit switch, effectively toggling the direction signal and reversing the stepper motor's rotation. To optimize user comfort and enhance blood flow, a strategically placed lexan sheet on top of the enclosure evenly distributes the roller's force across a larger surface area. This design minimizes overall pressure on the user while maintaining the beneficial pressure wave effect.
Known Issues And Solutions The first and most obvious Issue with the design is the height at which the box sits in the chair. While this is less than ideal the purpose of our project was to design a proof of concept using primarily off the shelf components. With this restriction we used a readily available metal enclosure that we were confident would be strong enough to support the user however the heights available were less than ideal. Another known issue is that if the maximum safe stepper motor speed is set too high when the limit switch is triggered it can lead to an issue where the stepper motor will slow down to a fraction of it's set speed. This issue was discovered extremely late into development and as a result we were unable to be accurately diagnosed. Although by reducing the maximum speed to a more reasonable and safe value the issue was resolved.
