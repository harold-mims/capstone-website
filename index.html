Identifying The Problem
Background Throughout the United States, there are over 3.3 million wheelchair users, with 1.8 million being aged 65 and older. For these individuals, bedsores are a very common issue that if left unchecked, can cause death. Elderly wheelchair users are at risk for developing bedsores due to their limited and restricted mobility. The same applies to chronic wheelchair users such as disabled veterans. The pain from bedsores also prevents users from using wheelchairs, causing them to be bedridden which will likely worsen their condition. The comfortable use of wheelchairs is necessary for the disabled populace to engage in daily activities and retain human dignity.
Problem Statement Full-time heelchair users are at risk for developing bedsores. Existing bedsores also cause pain and trouble in everyday activities. We believe that there are no effective existing solutions for wheelchairs to prevent bedsores.
Our Solution
Project Objective To design and implement a system for the active prevention and passive treatment of bedsores through the use of a roller mechanism to encourage blood flow through high risk areas.
Deliverables A functional and contained system that the user can add to an existing chair. This system will be able to communicate with the user's phone to control the rate of the roller mechanism remotely.
